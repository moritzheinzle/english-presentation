What is AI?

  • Definition: Artificial intelligence (AI) is technology that enables computers and machines to simulate human learning, comprehension, problem solving, decision making, creativity and autonomy.
  • Examples of AI:
  • Virtual Assistants (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant)
  • Recommendation Systems (Netflix, Amazon)
  • Autonomous Vehicles (Self-driving cars)
  • AI in Healthcare (Medical image analysis, disease prediction)
  • Large Language Models (GPT-4o)

Brief History of AI

  • 1956: AI field established at the Dartmouth Conference.
  • 1960s: Early AI programs like ELIZA and SHRDLU emerge.
  • 1970s–80s: "AI Winter" slows progress; expert systems rise.
  • 1997: IBM’s Deep Blue defeats Kasparov in chess.
  • 2012: Deep learning gains prominence with AlexNet.
  • 2016: AlphaGo beats Go champion Lee Sedol.
  • 2020s: Large language models (GPT) and creative AI tools (DALL·E) thrive.

Why is AI Important Today?

  • Ubiquity and Impact: AI is transforming industries like healthcare, automotive, finance, and entertainment.
  • Economic Importance: AI is expected to drive massive economic growth, with the AI market forecasted to reach over $190 billion by 2025.
  • Everyday Life: From powering virtual assistants to recommendation engines, AI is integrated into daily activities like shopping and navigation.

Supervised Learning

    • Linear Regression: Predicts continuous values based on a linear relationship between input and output.
    • Logistic Regression: Used for binary classification, predicting probabilities with a logistic function.
